# Photography

> **Owner:** Brand Creative Direction
> **Last updated:** 2026-03-26
> **Status:** Live

Photography at Qonto follows a "colorful reality" style. As an everyday partner to entrepreneurs, we celebrate their work, not the polish. We show real people in their natural environments, capturing authentic moments that tell their stories. Every image should feel honest, warm, and intentional.

This territory covers both traditional photography direction and AI-generated image guidelines. The rules are the same — the tools are different.

---

## Principles

- **Clarity** — high definition details, meaningful composition, every element should be intentional
- **Competence** — subjects are shown in their element, demonstrating mastery over their craft
- **Transparency** — honest lighting and environments, no artificial staging or "perfect" setups
- **Authenticity over aspiration** — real people, real workplaces, real moments
- **Diversity as default** — a range of individuals across ages, roles, and industries is intentionally represented

---

## Rules — shared

These apply to all photography regardless of context or production method.

### Protagonists are real people

Emphasize creating real people with authentic emotions in both their facial expressions and personal styling. Avoid overly polished or staged looks.

**Authenticity:** A diverse range of individuals, roles, and ages is intentionally represented, reflecting inclusivity and authenticity in modern work settings.

**Facial expressions:** Capture genuine, unposed moments. Natural expressions, subtle smiles, and authentic reactions are preferred over forced smiles. Approachable, focused, quietly confident.

**Personal styling:** Emphasize functional workwear with authentic details — scuffs, worn denim, personal accessories, and practical layers. Fabrics: linen, cotton, soft natural materials. Style: casual professional or creative freelancer. Deep, saturated accent colors as wardrobe pops.

### Mid-work posture

Natural, unposed body language, weight shifts, and the "interrupted action" look. Capture life mid-motion.

- Natural weight shifts and posture
- Interrupted action, mid-motion
- Avoid perfect symmetry or posing

### Authentic space

Environments should feel true to the situation and not overstaged. The amount of details and busyness should match the story we are telling.

**Texture over cleanliness:** Concrete, metal, raw wood, industrial surfaces.

**The "clutter" factor:** Tools, sketches, coffee cups, cables. Authentic mess.

**Realness:** Real workplaces for real representations.

### Lighting

Soft, directional natural light from large windows is the brand standard. Clear side or ¾ angle. Shadows present but never harsh. Skin texture and materials remain visible.

- Primary source: natural window light
- Direction: side-lit or ¾ angle
- Soft shadows that add depth without drama
- Pure white balance mimicking midday daylight (5500K)
- No overly visible artificial lighting fixtures

### Color and tone

The only saturated colors are deep accent tones on clothing and accessories. Backgrounds are strictly neutral, muted, and earthy.

- Global contrast: medium-high, punchy and dimensional
- Base palette: soft, muted, earthy tones across the whole image
- Saturated colors: reserved exclusively for clothing accents and accessories
- Backgrounds: neutral and muted earthy tones, grays, or off-whites
- Natural skin tones throughout
- No cinematic color grading

### Camera and lens

- Focal length: 35mm to 50mm
- Aperture: starting at f/5.6 (can go higher on close-ups)
- Shallow to medium depth of field with clear subject separation
- Natural, slight lens softness — not clinical sharpness
- Framing: the viewer should feel "in the room"

## Photography modes

### Lifestyle portrait

Presenting people as the protagonists.

**Establish a connection:** Subjects look directly into the camera to create a personal connection with the viewer, becoming the focal point as the main character.

**Background:** A work background supports the subject with minimal visual interference. It is softly blurred or used as a framing element.

### Narrative photography

Portraying people in the context of their work, capturing them in action.

**Subject mid-action:** Subjects are photographed fully immersed in their tasks, looking at their work rather than the camera.

**Authentic story:** The emphasis is on telling the authentic story of their business, process, and passion through natural actions and real moments.

### Product in use

Devices and physical products in action in real-world scenarios (in-hand, in-pocket, on counters), with natural gestures emphasizing real, meaningful interaction.

**Here for your business:** Qonto is emphasizing real meaningful interaction in day to day lives of Qonto customers.

**Up-to-date:** The Qonto app is always shown in real context, using the latest devices and physical product versions in circulation.

### The product is present

Devices and physical products placed in real-world environments, showing how Qonto is present during everyday work life, but never in the way of work.

**Qonto is always with you:** Subjects are absent or fully immersed in their tasks, while Qonto product is shown on the side, in their working space.

**Authentic space:** The Qonto app is presented surrounded by real work environment — tools, sketches, coffee cups, cables, work in progress.

## Rules — marketing

Marketing photography follows all shared rules above, plus:

**Palette:** True-to-life. Natural saturation. Colors should be vibrant but grounded in reality. Touches of Qonto color hues must be used whenever possible.

**Light bounce:** Natural environment fill. Utilize natural bounce from environment surfaces (concrete, metal, wood) to subtly fill shadows while maintaining high contrast. Avoid artificial studio reflectors.

**Technical note:** Maintain sharp focus across the subject. Background should not be blurred too much — it should help identify the kind of business and give some context.

---

## Rules — product

- Use functional photography only in product contexts
- The Qonto app is always shown using the latest UI and real data
- Devices should be current models
- Product photography follows the same lighting and color rules as marketing

## Negative cues (never do this)

### Technical
- Golden hour lighting
- Sepia tones
- Bokeh overload
- Heavy grain
- Flat lighting
- Soft focus (in narrative/functional shots)

### Subject and styling
- Staged smiles or forced enthusiasm
- Corporate stiffness or headshot energy
- Stock-photo aesthetic
- Perfect, symmetrical, model-like faces
- Overly dramatic or editorial expressions
- Skin with texture that looks AI-generated
- Suits or formal business attire (unless business-specific)
- Loud patterns or busy prints

### Environment
- Plain studio backdrops
- Cluttered or visually noisy backgrounds
- Backgrounds with too-strong saturated colors
- Backgrounds that draw more attention than the subject

## AI image generation

When generating photography with AI tools (Midjourney, Nano Banana Pro, Adobe Firefly), use the rules above as the creative direction and the prompt structures below as the technical execution.

### Prompt anatomy

A well-structured prompt covers these dimensions. Missing any dimension gives the AI room to default to generic choices.

1. **Subject** — who is in the image (age, gender, hair, one or two features). Don't over-specify.
2. **Expression and mood** — pair an expression with a body language cue: "soft smile, arms loosely crossed"
3. **Styling and wardrobe** — name the fabric and the color explicitly: "a mint-colored linen shirt"
4. **Lighting** — always define direction and quality: "soft natural light from a large window to the left"
5. **Color and tone** — separate where saturated colors are allowed from where they are not: "deep saturated color on clothing" and "neutral off-white or gray background"
6. **Environment** — mention one or two specific background elements to make blur feel real: "in a pottery studio, tools on shelves behind"
7. **Lens and camera** — simulate settings: "shot on 35mm lens, f/1.8 aperture, shallow depth of field"
8. **Negative cues** — always include to suppress AI defaults

### Midjourney template

```
{subject.gender}, {subject.age}, {subject.hair}, {subject.feature},
{expression.mood}, {expression.body},
wearing {wardrobe.color} {wardrobe.fabric} {wardrobe.layer},
{wardrobe.accessory},
shot on 35mm lens, f/1.8 aperture, shallow depth of field,
natural lens rendering,
soft natural window light from the side, diffused daylight,
neutral white balance, soft shadows, photorealistic,
muted earthy color palette, deep saturated clothing,
neutral off-white background, medium contrast, natural skin tones,
no cinematic color grading,
{environment.space} background, {environment.detail},
softly blurred background, neutral muted tones
--no warm filter, golden hour, extreme bokeh, studio solid background, perfect skin, corporate stock photo, forced smile, soft focus, sepia tones, heavy grain --style raw --ar {aspect.ratio}
```

**Midjourney syntax rules:** All `--` parameters must be grouped at the very end of the prompt. Never place `--style raw` inline with descriptive text — Midjourney will parse everything after `--style` as part of that flag. Use `--ar 4:5` for social, `--ar 16:9` for hero banners, `--ar 1:1` for square formats.

### Global negative prompt (always include)

**For Midjourney** — use the `--no` flag at the end of the prompt:
```
--no warm filter, golden hour, extreme bokeh, studio solid background, perfect skin, corporate stock photo, forced smile, soft focus, sepia tones, heavy grain
```

**For Nano Banana Pro and Firefly** — include as natural language in the prompt body:
```
no warm filter, no golden hour, no extreme bokeh,
no completely blurry background, no studio solid background,
no perfect skin, no corporate stock photo, no empty space,
no forced smile, no looking at camera (for narrative shots)
```

### Quick reference — positive technical prompts

Editorial photography, 5500K daylight, crisp details, industrial textures, natural skin tones, foreground depth, functional clutter

### Quick reference — negative technical prompts

Golden hour, sepia tones, bokeh overload, heavy grain, staged smiles, flat lighting, stock-photo aesthetic, soft focus

## Story example: meet Maya

A complete character example showing how all rules come together.

**Character:** Amaya, 31 years old, Spanish-Moroccan heritage. She's creating a food and spicery shop that will produce her homemade sauces in jars and some tapas specialities.

**Scenario:** Behind the main counter of her shop, mid-conversation with a customer, gesturing toward a shelf. Leaning forward over the counter, right arm extended pointing off-camera. Natural expression — mid-speech, eyebrows slightly raised, warm but not posed. The background shows white and green shelving with sauce jars and family photos. A customer's blurred shoulder is in the foreground, establishing the interaction.

**Lighting:** Neutral daylight 5500K. Large window from customer-facing side providing soft fill. Soft directional light with natural shadows.

**Camera:** 50mm, f/5.6. Sharp focus on face and pointing hand. Customer in foreground naturally blurred. Slightly low angle (chest height) from customer's perspective.

**Color:** True-to-life, neutral, high fidelity. Medium-high contrast, natural shadows preserved. No vintage, no sepia, no teal-and-orange.

**Style keywords:** Authentic editorial, workplace documentary, realistic skin texture, high-resolution, anti-stock photo, environmental portrait, candid interaction.
